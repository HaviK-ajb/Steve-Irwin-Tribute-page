Steve Irwin

22/2/1962 - 4/9/2006

Steve Irwin's timeline:

  • 1962 - Born in Essendon, a suburban area located in Melbourne, Austrailia.
  • 1970 - Moved to Queensland where he would attend school. He also wrestled his first crocodile at age 9.
  • 1991 - First met Terri Raines, an American naturalist from Eugene, Oregon, who was visiting wildlife rehabilitation facilities in Australia and had decided to visit the zoo. They were later engaged.
  • 1992 - Steve took over the management of the Queensland's East Coast Crocodile Management program. He renamed the program as the "Autralia Zoo". The zoo later was later voted as the top tourist destination for Queensland which would attract American tourists to visit Australia.
  • 1996 - Steve and Terry filmed the first episode of The Crocodile Hunter which was during their honeymoon while they were trapping crocodiles. The programming would later debut on Australian TV networks.
  • 1997 - The Crocodile Hunter makes its way onto American Television. This would become a great success all throughout the United States, the UK, and over 130 other countries. reaching 500 million people.
  • 1998 - Steve and his wife gives birth to their daughter and first child Bindi Irwin.
  • 2001 - Steve appears in a cameo role in the Eddie Murphy film Dr. Dolittle 2.
  • 2002 - Steve stars in his own film The Crocodile Hunter: Collision Course. Despite its mixed reviews he won an award for Best Family Feature Film for a comedy film at the Young Artist Awards ceremony.
  • 2003 - Steve and his wife give birth to their second child and son Bob Irwin.
  • 2004 - He was appointed ambassador for The Ghan, a passenger train running from Adelaide to Alice Springs in the central Australian outback.
  • 2005 - He played as a voice actor in the animated film Happy Feet as an elephant seal named Trev.
  • 2006 - Steve Irwin dies at age 44 from stingray puncture to his chest. His funeral was later held in the Australia Zoo where he was also buried.
